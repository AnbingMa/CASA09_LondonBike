Daily cycle changed flow in three years

The Lockdown measures legally come into force in March 2020 and the ‘Stay at home’ order ends in March 2021. One day at the beginning of April was chosen to reflect public cycling usage during the pandemic and lockdown

The public cycle usage has followed a similar Multimodal distribution for the typical day in 2019 and 2021. Two peaks fit exactly with the morning rush hours from 7:00 to 9:00 and afternoon rush hours from 16:00 to 19:00. The flows in 2021 decreased to only half of the flows in 2019 in the morning peak time while it decreased only one-third in the afternoon peak time.

In 2020, the number of rides decreases approximately by four-fifths for the morning rush hours and the high usage period is significantly different which only peaks around afternoon rush hour. It could be due to the lockdown measures that all people work from home and people may cycle for exercise after dinner which could partly explain why it still peaks from 16:00 to 19:00.
