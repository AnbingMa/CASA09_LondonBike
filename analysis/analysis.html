Demand on different tubes in 2019 vs 2020

Demand on the main transport

Different variations in the use of different modes of transport also occurred during COVID-19. After the first lock down, all transport modes except the bicycle showed a clear downward trend, while the bicycle showed a clear upward trend. This is probably due to the fear of the epidemic and the announcement of the lock down policy, which led to a reduction in unnecessary commuting and the choice of transport with less contact with others. During the closure period, parks open to the public have also become an option for people to exercise by bicycle. As policies and measures for COVID-19 gradually increase and Londoners return to their pre-epidemic state, the use of bicycles gradually re-emerges as a different option for citizens along with other modes of transport.



Demand on different tubes in 2019 vs 2020

Travel purpose on 2019 vs 2020

In 2020/21 there was an increase in the share of cycling duration associated with private transport trips (increasing from 0 percent to 3 percent) and also as part of walking trips (O percent to 11 percent) meaning that although the majority of cycling duration was still done as cycle all the way trips, the proportion decreased from 98 percent in 2019/20 to 84 percent in 2020/21.

The figure shows that the purposes for which London residents cycled have changed as a result of the pandemic. The share of cycling duration associated with commuting trips decreased from just less than half (45 percent) to 29 percent and for other work-related trips, it decreased from 14 percent to just 3 percent. The pattern is also the same as walking for shopping or personal business and leisure trips, which both saw an increase in the share of cycling duration (from 7 percent to 19 percent for shopping/personal business and from 26 percent to 42 percent for leisure). Showing that the reduction in commuter cycling as a result of workplace restrictions has been compensated by an increase in home-based leisure trips (a "purpose shift').

Daily Flow/duration

Daily cycle changed flow in three years

As the Lockdown measures legally come into force in March 2020 and the ‘Stay at home’ order ends in March 2021, we choose one day at the beginning of April as our typical date to represent these three years to reflect the situation of public cycling usage before the pandemic (2019), during the pandemic and lockdown (2020) and post lockdown (2021).

The public cycle usage has shown a similar trend for the typical day in 2019 and 2021. During the day, they follow a Multimodal distribution, and two peaks fit exactly with the morning rush hours from 7:00 to 9:00 and afternoon rush hours from 16:00 to 18:00. It is because people will need to use bicycles as their transport mode to commute during these two rush hours. The flows in 2021 decreased to only half of the flows in 2019 in the morning peak time while it decreased only one-third in the afternoon peak time.

And it is noticeable that in 2020, the number of rides decreases approximately by four-fifths for the morning rush hours and the high usage period is significantly different which only peaks around afternoon rush hour. The reason behind this pattern could be due to the lockdown measures that all people work from home which reduces the demand for the cycle as a tool for commuting in the morning. And cycling may become a physical activity for exercise and recreation after dinner which could partly explain why it still peaks from 16:00 to 18:00.



Daily Flow/duration

Daily cycle changed travel time in three years

Except for the daily flows, we are also curious about the total duration of cycle usage. From the picture, the total usage time of the public cycle still follows the same pattern of the flow count with two peaks at rush hour in 2019 and 2021 while only peaks around afternoon rush hour in 2020.

In addition, bicycle travel time during morning peak hours has also been reduced to about half of the pre-pandemic total in 2019. However, the difference is that the total riding time in the afternoon peak hours in 2020 has exceeded that in both 2019 and 2021, which may be because according to the lockdown policies, non-essential contact and travel are restricted while parks are opened for outdoor walking and cycling. The possibility that citizens choose cycling as an outdoor activity to exercise and stay physical fitness helps explain the much larger increase in cycling from 16:00 to 18:00 during the pandemic period compared to before the pandemic.

Considering the usage of cycling will be different on the weekday and w eekends, we choose a typical week in three years to visualize the various distributions. The flows and durations for the weekday are summed up and divided by five days to get the mean value that represents a typical weekday. The flows and durations for the weekend are summed up and divided by two days to get the mean value that reflects a typical weekend.

From the picture, the weekend flow shows a significantly different pattern than the weekday. The rush hour peaks are still prominent on the weekend which increases fast between lunch and dinner time, from 12:00 to 17:00, while the peak travel time on weekdays is during commute time. And journeys on weekend shows that the highest number of flows happened in 2020 and then 2021, suggesting the increasing demand for cycling during and post the pandemic. The number of weekend travels in 2020 is more than twice that of 2019. This may be because cycling become a popular mode of transportation for urban travel to avoid being infected throughout the epidemic. Therefore, we predict it could also play a critical part in post-epidemic urban transportation since people may get customed to using cycles.

Reression analysis

Bicycle day flow & COVID-19 new cases

Regression analysis was implemented to examine whether the number of COVID-19 infections had an effect on people's choice of bicycle trips, and all the variables are normalized in the same range to compare the independent variable impact through coefficient. The analysis showed that the number of COVID-19 infections was negatively correlated with the flow of bicycle trips, taking into account weather conditions, but this impact was small compared with weather factors.

On the other hand, It is found that the bicycle travel flow and daily covid-19 infection are both increased in the covid-19 period. So it could be supposed that other factors like policies, limited the COVID-19 impact on bicycle travel.



Time prediction model – SARIMAX

Date Flow

According to the date range of datasets from 2020-02-27 to 2021-03-31, we can see there’s a seasonal fluctuation during the period. After releasing the seasonal feature by diff 30 and one more time, we set a SARIMAX (8, 1, 4)× (3, 1, 0, 30) time prediction model.(Through Augmented Dicky-Fuller Unit Root Test, the result of ADF was -10.31, less than ‘1%’-3.45, ‘5%’-2.87, and ‘10%’-2.57. P-value was 3.11e-12, which means the model was stable. The residual was flattered around 0. Normal Q-Q plot also shown liner distributed. Durbin-Watson test the result was 1.997, which was close to 2.)

For the date range of datasets from 2021-04-01 to 2021-07-31, we set it as a test range, the uncertain was set 95%, the one-step forward line behaved perfectly to the real data trend. Thus, we can step one more step to the future using flow of bike in London.
